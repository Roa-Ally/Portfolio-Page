Language Quest

Overview

Language Quest is a story driven 2D adventure game that teaches Spanish by weaving language directly into gameplay rather than separating it into lessons. Players explore a world inspired by cultural storytelling, where understanding the language becomes essential to progressing through the narrative.


Instead of memorizing vocabulary in isolation, players learn through interaction by solving puzzles, engaging in dialogue, and reconstructing parts of the story using Spanish. Each word is tied to meaningful moments, making the learning experience feel natural and memorable rather than forced. The game focuses on building an emotional connection to both the language and the world, turning learning into something immersive, personal, and story focused.

Process

Language Quest was developed as my senior capstone project, and it was my first time leading a full team. I led four developers who had no prior experience with Unity, which meant the process was not just about building the game but also guiding the team through learning Unity, C#, and game development from the ground up.


We started by defining the core systems like dialogue, puzzles, and player interaction, then built them step by step while continuously testing and refining. I organized the workflow, assigned tasks, and made sure everyone stayed aligned, while also helping troubleshoot issues and teach concepts as they came up. The process was very hands on and collaborative, with a lot of learning happening in real time.


As development progressed, we integrated systems like the dialogue manager, language toggle, and puzzle mechanics, continuously testing to make sure everything worked together smoothly. The experience was very collaborative and growth focused, turning a group of beginners into a team capable of delivering a complete, functional game while learning along the way.


Alongside leading development, I was also responsible for the game’s visual design, creating the environmental art, UI, and overall aesthetic of the world (excluding the main character). This helped shape the tone of the experience and bring the narrative to life visually.


Overall, the project was both a technical and leadership experience, turning a team of beginners into a fully functioning development team and delivering a complete, story driven game.
