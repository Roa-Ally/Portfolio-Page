Elucid

Overview

Elucid is a dark fantasy puzzle experience that drops players into a haunting, fragmented void where logic is shaped by light itself. Using mirrors to bend and redirect beams, players must navigate intricate environments and unlock paths forward by solving spatial challenges.


Rather than relying on fast action, the game leans into atmosphere and tension, encouraging careful thinking and experimentation. Each puzzle feels like uncovering a piece of a larger mystery, as players gradually reveal the story of the Formless and the world hidden within the darkness. The combination of minimal guidance, eerie visuals, and light based mechanics creates a quiet but immersive journey focused on discovery and problem solving.

Tools

Unity 2D

Aseprite

Process

The development of Elucid focused heavily on designing and refining puzzle mechanics before anything else. Instead of starting with action or player abilities, the team began by experimenting with how light could interact with mirrors in a way that felt intuitive but still challenging. Early prototypes were used to test beam behavior, reflection angles, and how players would understand cause and effect within each puzzle.


Once the core system felt solid, level design became the main focus. Puzzles were carefully crafted to introduce mechanics gradually, allowing players to learn through interaction rather than instructions. A lot of time went into iterating on layouts, adjusting difficulty, and making sure each solution felt satisfying to discover rather than obvious or frustrating.


Visuals and atmosphere were developed alongside the puzzles to support the experience. Lighting, color, and minimal UI were used intentionally to guide attention and create a sense of mystery without overwhelming the player. Playtesting played a big role in shaping the final experience, helping refine clarity, pacing, and how players interpret each puzzle.


Overall, the process was more design driven and experimental, centered around building a cohesive, immersive puzzle experience rather than a fast paced gameplay loop.
