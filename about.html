About Me

I’m a senior Computer Science student at FIU, and one thing I’ve really learned is that getting better at anything comes from messing up first. There’s a quote I like that has stuck with me is, “We learn to walk by falling down. If we never fell down, we would never walk.”


I got into programming before I ever stepped into leadership. I built a few projects on my own early on, and honestly, a lot of it was just trial and error. I’d get stuck on bugs for hours, try random fixes, break things even more, and then eventually figure it out. It wasn’t always fun in the moment, but that’s how I learned to actually understand what I was doing instead of just copying solutions. Over time, I got better at being patient, thinking through problems, and learning from every mistake.


What I enjoy most is building something from nothing and watching it slowly come together. I’ve worked across game development, front end, and interactive systems, and I like being able to move between those spaces. During my time at Universal Creative, I worked with both the Virtual Attractions team and the IXP Interactive Experience and Play team. I helped build tools, worked in Unity, used React for front end work, and created 3D models, animations, and digital twins in Maya and Unity. That experience really pushed me to be adaptable and pick up new tools quickly.
